cosmic constellation, full-bleed

3 starfield layers · restrained palette · floating type · series-A polish bar

The constellation owns the viewport. Three star layers parallax at different speeds, creating real depth. The central anchor is a fully-bloomed orb with three concentric pulse rings — this is the visual focal point that the eye lands on first. Six clusters orbit it with intentional hierarchy: foundations and career are closest (largest, brightest), networking gets the single cyan accent so the eye notices it, technical and experience are recessed (smaller, dimmer), resources is the farthest (faintest). Palette discipline: violet primary, cyan as the one accent, no rainbow.

Typography floats inside the space rather than sitting on top of it. The eyebrow ("AN OPEN WIKI · BY PROGSU") in tight-tracked mono. Headline in big italic serif with multi-layer drop-shadow so it stays legible against starfields. Sub-line names the offer types without flexing numbers. Search bar in the lower-third with backdrop-blur glass effect and a subtle purple glow border — modeled on Linear's command palette aesthetic.

No masthead, no boxed framing, no nav competing for attention. Just: tiny logo top-left, sparse nav top-right, the wiki itself in the middle of the screen as a living constellation. Below the fold (not shown here): the numbered ToC + featured guides + contribute footer. This hero is the front door; the editorial structure lives below.

references the bar is set against: Linear (precision, command-palette aesthetic), Vercel (depth + restraint), Anthropic (calm authority), Modal (3D dimensional), Resend (typography + atmospheric bg), Phantom (cosmic gradients done right). All restrained on color, generous with space, polished on motion.

what's still abstract in this mockup: mouse-parallax on the star layers, subtle motion when you hover a cluster (its sub-nodes pulse and a tooltip card appears), node-to-tooltip transition when you click. Those are real-build details that don't render in a static HTML preview but absolutely ship in the React/Astro version.

push back if: typography is in the wrong place (above-the-fold vs in space), palette is too monochrome (want more cluster differentiation), anchor is too big/small, search should be at the top instead of bottom, copy needs different framing.
